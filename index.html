Education is one of the most essential component of life. It is also an effective element to facilitate advancement and change. Education is the essential foundation of life, that gives one the tools to live effectively and substantially. The absents of education limits the potential of life itself. This belief informs my approach and drives my teaching philosophy. It is imperative to be aware of these tools and how to apply it to life and the social work profession.

My teaching philosophy focuses on educating and promoting one’s intellect by increasing knowledge base, self-esteem and critical thinking which subsequently improve standards of living. I believe that educators have the most important job in shaping and developing growing minds. My objectives as an educator is to foster critical thinking skills, to facilitate the acquisition of lifelong learning skills, to help students develop evidence-based clinical problem-solving strategies, and to prepare students to function as highly skilled and competent critical thinkers.

My teaching approach focuses on two main principles, (1) Active Student Learning which strongly influences students-learning outcome and (2) Assessment Procedures which strongly influence students acquisition of knowledge. In order to maximize students learning, I employ different methods and techniques of teaching such as lectures, class discussions, active learning, cooperative learning and integrated technology. The main methods of assessing student learning are testing, observation and active classroom participation.

My teaching philosophy encompasses a multicultural education approach. This is a progressive approach for transforming education, based on educational equality and social justice. “Multiculturalism” in an educational setting can be an “Achilles” heel for novice or even an experienced educator. It is imperative that an educator be culturally competent in the classroom. To have a knowledge of students’ ethnicity and culture allows an educator to connect with the students more effectively. Students and their life’s history and experiences should be placed at the center of the teaching and learning process.
